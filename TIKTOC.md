## Concept
Volume 3 teaches the student to **attack problems with no closed-form solution — perturbation theory, the variational method, WKB, and scattering — and model real atoms, fields, and solids**, with the +1 simulation layer carried from Volume 1. Course textbook; chapter = week; read in sequence.

**Target course:** Junior–senior QM II / methods course. 15-week map — chapters across Weeks 1–11, with the remaining weeks for the capstone project, review, and exam.

## The +1 Simulation System
Each chapter ends with an LLM Exercise block: the `CLAUDE.md` coding constitution, a copy-paste four-move D3 simulation prompt for the chapter's core object, exploration tasks, and an extension prompt. The Brutalist governing files are generated in Volume 1, Chapter 0 and travel with the student.

## Three-Act Arc
- Act One (1–4): stationary methods — perturbation theory, fine structure, variational, WKB.
- Act Two (5–8): dynamics and scattering — transitions, radiation, cross-sections, Born.
- Act Three (9–11): real systems — atoms in fields, solids, a modeling capstone.
