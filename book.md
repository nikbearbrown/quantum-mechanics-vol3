# Quantum Mechanics Vol. 3: Approximation Methods and Real Systems

**Author:** Nik Bear Brown

---

## One-Sentence Pitch
The simulation-first undergraduate volume that teaches the student to attack problems with no closed-form solution — perturbation theory, the variational method, WKB, and scattering — and model real atoms, fields, and solids.

## The Argument
Quantum mechanics is hard because its objects have no classical analogue and the standard textbook gives no way to build intuition for them. This volume pairs every concept with an LLM-generated, student-modifiable D3 simulation, so the student sees each new object before manipulating its algebra. It is Volume 3 of the *Quantum Mechanics* series.

## The Gap
Griffiths/Townsend are rigorous but static; video and applets are visual but non-rigorous and unmodifiable. This volume gives the student a third object: a simulation they built, governed by physics they can derive, that they can change and watch respond.

## The Reader
Junior–senior QM II / methods course. Enters with the prerequisites carried from the prior volume (or, for Volume 1, ODEs, complex numbers, and introductory mechanics/waves). Wants to *do* quantum mechanics, not just read about it. The optional **Quantum Mechanics Vol. 5 — Math for Quantum Physics** companion supplies any math refresher, indexed to the chapter the student is on.

## High-Level Outline
- Act One (1–4): stationary methods — perturbation theory, fine structure, variational, WKB.
- Act Two (5–8): dynamics and scattering — transitions, radiation, cross-sections, Born.
- Act Three (9–11): real systems — atoms in fields, solids, a modeling capstone.
